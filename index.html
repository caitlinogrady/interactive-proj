Visualization 2 Redesigned

Caption for Visualization 2:

This is a scatter plot of the proportion of white students at the school compared to the teacher-student ratio at said school. There is also a trendline for the overall scatterplot, with the slope of the line printed next to it. Some changes were made to the original visualization in that extreme outliers were ignored (aka teacher-student ratios that were above 50:1), in order to make the trend among the points easier to see and more representive of the nature of the majority of the points. It is made to be interactive with two sliders at the bottom, that allow you to choose a certain bottom threshold for teacher-student proportion and for white student population proportion. These sliders will then highlight the points that fall within the desired range.

Visualization 3 Redesigned

Caption for Visualization 3

This are two scatterplots, each showing the Math and ELA standardized testing results for NY Public School students, and the plots show the proportion of students at a school who reached a certain testing level compared to the proportion of white students at the school. The changes made to this visualization were that a trendline was added to each plot in order to see the overall trend of the points and understand the general direction of change. This visualization was made to be interactive by adding a dropdown menu at the bottom, where the user can choose which level of testing they want to focus the charts on. For example, choosing level 1 will change both the math and the ELA plots to show the proportion of students who reached level 1 against the proportion of white students at the school.
